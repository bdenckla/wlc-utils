Major Changes for this Release

·        WLC 4.22 had more changes relative to its predecessor than were seen in previous releases because of the length of its development cycle (four and a half years). Going forward we are committed to returning to more frequent releases. In the coming months we are also going to release the WLC under a “Creative Commons” license.

In describing our changes, we will use the term “sets of changes.” Some of those “sets” will be a single change to a single word in the WLC, a few will be changes to dozens of related words, and others somewhere in between.

In this release there were 7 textual changes, 35 changes of a bracket note with a textual change, 21 changes in hypothetical Ketiv pointing, 25 additions of a “]1” bracket note; 20 additions of a “]U” bracket note; 13 additions of a “]e” bracket note; 8 additions of a “]p” bracket note; 14 additions of a “]w” bracket note; 32 changes from a “]1” bracket note to “]U”; 10 changes from “]1” bracket note to “]s” (or from “]U” to “]S]s”); and 25 other changes of bracket notes without a textual change.

Two words were swapped in a complex Ketiv-Qere reading in Ezra 4:12, and a regular word was converted into a Ketiv-Qere pair of words in Daniel 2:39.

In addition, we have removed all twelve occurrences of the Rafe (“,”) from our text. Rafes are extremely common in the Leningrad Codex, but only 12 are found in BHS. None are found in extant fascicles of BHQ or in Aron Dotan’s Biblia Hebraica Leningradensia (BHL) (Hendrickson Publishers, 2001). We have decided to follow BHQ and BHL at this time in ignoring all Rafes.

Textual Corrections

We have also made several changes to the text of WLC in places where we found we did not match the Leningrad Codex. This sometimes (but not always) causes us to disagree with BHS (and/or BHQ). This release includes a number of changes to Ketiv-Qere readings as well as several changes to consonants, vowels, punctuation, and accents. These changes usually cause us to disagree with BHS, though occasionally a change actually causes us to remove a disagreement with BHS. A review of the Genesis fascicle of BHQ led to the change of a number of “]1” bracket notes (indicating that we agree with BHS on an unexpected reading) to “]U” (indicating that we agree with both BHS and BHQ on an unexpected reading) as well as other changes and corrections.

We also added four new bracket notes: “]e” to mark unexpected or unusual readings that do not rise to the level of an “error” in the Leningrad Codex, “]s” to mark occurrences of a printing problem in BHS when a particular accent is found on a final Lamed in a word, “]S” to mark occurrences of the same printing problem as “]s” in extant fascicles of BHQ (so far these always occur with “]s”), and “]w” to indicate unusual Qere forms that are abbreviated in the margins of the Leningrad Codex.

Many users will not be interested in most or all of these new bracket notes, but they are provided to add additional text critical information to our electronic text of the Leningrad Codex. Some users will find that the “]e” bracket note on a word useful because it will confirm their suspicion that the Leningrad Codex has an unusual reading at that point.
